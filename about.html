About Me...

Currently, I am working on my Master's Degree in User Experience Design at the VCU Brandcenter. I graduated with a B.S. in Information Systems from the Virginia Commonwealth University in May 2020. With a diverse background in business, digital marketing, web development, design, and research, I value both creative innovation and strategic problem-solving.

Outside of school, I'm volunteering full time as a User Experience Designer for a global-nonprofit organization: BAPS Charities.

I am currently seeking full-time/ internship opportunities in UX/ Product Design starting Summer 2020.

My Story...

Growing up in a traditional Asian family, it was challenging to pursue a creative path non-conforming to the stereotypes of success. Discovering graphic design in high school, I was intrigued by the systematic quality of graphics as a tool for communication unlike fine arts abstraction. I practiced creating flyers, logos, and vector illustrations that were aesthetically pleasing and admirable to the eyes of others, but always wondered if design could be a service to make greater impact. After discovering user experience & product design, I realized that design is a universal problem-solving process, much more to its aesthetics. It's a field where I can apply my entire skillset with projects and apply my passion for problem-solving and making an impact on peopleâ€™s lives.

My Goals...

As a designer, I aspire to create engaging, functional and accessible user experiences that delight people and solve complex business problems. With every learning opportunity, I strive to educate myself and improve in order to fully experience design's multidisciplinary culture.
