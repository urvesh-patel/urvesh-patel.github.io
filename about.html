NICE TO MEET YOU — I'M URVESH

I'm a senior at the Virginia Commonwealth University studying Information Systems & Design. Previously, I was a Product Design Intern at Agora Financial conducting research and synthesizing the data to create better web-application experiences. Currently, I'm volunteering full time as a User Experience Designer for a global-nonprofit organization: BAPS Charities. I am currently seeking full-time/ internship opportunities in UX/ Product Design starting Summer 2020.

Growing up in a traditional Asian family, it was challenging to pursue a creative path non-conforming to the stereotypes of success. Discovering graphic design in high school, I was intrigued by the systematic quality of graphics as a tool for communication unlike fine arts abstraction. I practiced creating flyers, logos, and vector illustrations that were aesthetically pleasing and admirable to the eyes of others, but always wondered if design could be a service to make greater impact. After attending VCU’s School of Business, I realized that design is a universal problem-solving process, much more to its aesthetics. For once, I found something I was deeply passionate about and excelled beyond expectations.

As a designer, I aspire to create engaging, functional and accessible user experiences that delight people and solve complex business problems. With every learning opportunity, I strive to educate myself and improve in order to fully experience design's multidisciplinary culture.
